Definiciones:
Recurrent AFib: A recurrence of AFib after self-termination or after successful treatment with anti-arrhythmic drugs, cardioversion or ablation
Paroxysmal AFib: An episode of AFib that self-terminates or terminates with intervention in ≤7 days
Persistent AFib: A sustained episode of AFib lasting > 7days that does not self-terminate
Permanent AFib: Irreversible AFib despite attempts to restore and/or maintain sinus rhythm
CPAP: Continuous Positive Airway Pressure is a therapeutic device used to treat obstructive sleep apnea. The treatment involves delivering pressurized air to the upper airway via a nasal mask.

For patients with AFib and moderate-severe OSA, we are interested in your current level of certainty about which of the following treatment options is better for reducing the risk of a number of patient outcomes.

For the treatment option you choose, please select the number (regardless of your reason), that corresponds with the strength of your certainty that the treatment is superior (5=very certain, 1=not very certain). For each outcome we are also interested in whether you would consider participating in a multi-centre randomized trial (if funding and research support were available).
Please select ‘Yes’ or ‘No’ for each outcome to indicate whether you would participate in an RCT in which patients would be randomly allocated to either treatment option.
